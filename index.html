Upfront Research

Welcome to our website.

Upfront Research is an independent market maker, and a financial research and services company.


We use technology, modeling, and research to identify opportunities across global financial markets. We provide near-continuous, competitive quotes for a wide range of assets. We act as a bridge between supply and demand, contributing to two-way markets efficiency and transparency across borders and time.

We capture opportunities, add liquidity, and strategically manage a short-term inventory of long and short positions across currencies, equities, and commodities. We execute trades at high velocity and low latency through proprietary trading systems developed entirely in-house. We continuously research and analyze data to refine our strategies and generate innovative ideas.

We actively manage market risk, automated execution risk, operational risk, and regulatory compliance. Our encrypted server network connects major financial centers including Chicago, New York, and London. Our core infrastructure is colocated in Ashburn, Virginia. We adhere to industry best practices and data protection regulations, and undergo independent reviews to minimize our environmental footprint and achieve carbon neutrality.


We focus on special situations within the US equity market. Our tools continuously monitor corporate announcements, regulatory filings and market data. We concentrate on making prudent arbitrage decisions, and prioritize risk mitigation and diversification. We do not sell or distribute research products.

Our coverage includes events such as activist campaigns, bankruptcies, carve-outs, Dutch auctions, index additions and deletions, insider transactions, legal proceedings, liquidations, listing changes, mergers and acquisitions, new IPOs, post bankruptcies, privatizations, recent management changes, secondary offerings, short campaigns, special dividends, spin-mergers, spin-offs, strategic alternatives, tender offers, upcoming unlocks.


We provide staff augmentation services to asset management firms and family offices, and support their investment goals as an objective third party. We offer sourcing, evaluation and monitoring services for alternative investments, and we enable the internalization of selected alternative investment strategies. Our hybrid model offers several advantages: superior liquidity, tighter controls, reduced costs, enhanced transparency.

We work in total independence, and are not acting as funds distributors or marketers. We aim for durable partnerships by doing the right thing, not the easiest thing. We aspire to become a trusted contact for our clients in their future dealings. We only serve professional and institutional clients.

We are registered in Switzerland with ARIF, a self-regulatory body recognized by the FINMA, and a member of the Swiss Register of Client Advisers. We comply with relevant customer data privacy regulations including the EU GDPR, the US CCPA, and the Swiss nFDPA. We adhere to ISO 20700:2017 and SOC 2 guidelines for consulting services and data management.

See: Disclosures, Ombudsman.


Upfront Research, LLC, is incorporated in Switzerland and was established in 2023. The company operates as a self-funded entity with no outside investors. Maintaining our own capital allows us to make quick decisions and pursue innovative strategies free from external constraints.

We value hard work, frugality and discretion. Our company distributes a portion of its income to support non-financial institutions.

FinSA Disclosures


Introduction

This FinSA Client Information Document informs you about how Upfront Research, LLC (hereinafter referred to as “Upfront Research”, “we” or “us”) applies certain requirements of the Swiss Financial Services Act (FinSA) as of December 12, 2023.

FinSA mainly regulates the provision of financial services and the offering of financial instruments and aims to strengthen investor protection. In terms of content, FinSA is closely aligned with European regulatory reforms and essentially covers the following areas: Strengthening investor protection; Transparency in the context of financial products; Organizational requirements for the provision of financial services.

This FinSA Client Information Document gives you an overview of the implementation of the FinSA Code of Conduct by Upfront Research. It supplements any contractual agreement between you and Upfront Research. In the event of contradictions, the respective contractual agreement takes precedence.

Client Segmentation

According to FinSA, all financial services providers are required to assign their clients to one of the following three segments: Private Clients, Professional Clients, Institutional Clients. Upfront Research does not offer the category of Private Clients.

Each segment is assigned a different level of investor protection, with regard to information obligations, suitability and adequacy tests, documentation and accountability obligations. Before we provide financial services to you, we will classify you as either a Professional Client or an Institutional Client based on the information available to us and inform you accordingly. Any segmentation carried out applies generally to all financial services and product classes of our relationship.

You can request to change your classification by making an opt-in or an opt-out declaration, which will also change the level of client protection and rules of conduct for the client relationship. The opt-in or opt-out declaration will not be legally binding for Upfront Research until a written confirmation has been given to you.

Appropriateness and Suitability

We rely on the collection of comprehensive and correct personal information from our clients to assess appropriateness and suitability. We rely on the information and statements that you or the person authorized by you provide. You or your authorized representative(s) are also required to immediately notify us of any change or update to your personal information that has been collected as the basis for performing the appropriateness or suitability test. Please note that without complete and accurate personal information from you, we will not be able to perform the appropriateness or suitability test.

For professional and institutional clients, FinSA requires only a limited suitability test of financial services providers providing investment advice or asset management services. Upfront Research can generally assume that these clients have the necessary knowledge and experience and that the investment risks associated with financial services are financially sustainable. In the case of professional clients, we are generally required to record the investment objectives and to carry out a limited suitability test only when providing asset management services or investment advice, taking into account the client portfolio.

Conflicts of interest

Upfront Research has implemented appropriate organizational and administrative measures to prevent or mitigate conflicts of interest that may arise from the provision of financial services and that could pose a risk to the interests of our clients or lead to a disadvantage.

A conflict of interest arises when our interests do not coincide with or might not in the future coincide with the interests of a client. This may put one party at a disadvantage to the benefit of the other party. Our policies and rules on conflicts of interest are appropriate for the nature and scope of our business. They enable us to identify, record, prevent, mitigate or disclose conflicts of interest. If disadvantages for clients cannot be excluded or can only be excluded with disproportionate effort, we will disclose this to you in a suitable manner.

A number of controls ensure that our policies and rules on conflicts of interest remain appropriate and are followed. Please contact us if you have any questions about our policies and rules to prevent conflicts of interest.
